What is the Nanjing Massacre?

The Nanjing Massacre was a brutal slaughter of Chinese cilivians in 1937 by the Imperial Japanese Army in the city of Nanjing, then the capital of China. Japan invaded China in 1931, and in December 1937, the Japanese Army captured Nanjing city. The Massacre started on December 13 and lasted for six weeks. Massive murders and rapes were commited, and about 300,000 people were tortured to death. In some literature, the incident is referred to as “the Rape of Nanking”, Nanking being the former English spelling of the city name.

The incident took place in 1937, but I used “during WW2” in the title, which may cause controversy since it’s widely known that WW2 started in 1939 and ended in 1945. Here’s my brief explanation: The war in which the Nanjing Massacre took place did not end until September 2, 1945, when Japan surrended unconditionally and WW2 came to an end. More importantly, at the same time Japan invaded not only China but several countries. Therefore I think it is legitimately part of World War 2. I honestly don’t see any reason why not.
